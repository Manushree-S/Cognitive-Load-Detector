# What is Cognitive Load?

Cognitive load simply means how much mental effort our brain uses while learning or doing a task. Every person has a limited capacity to process information. When too much information is presented at once, the brain becomes overloaded, making it difficult to concentrate, understand, or remember things.

For most students, this may only cause temporary difficulty. However, for students with ADHD and Dyslexia, high cognitive load can significantly affect their learning experience.

# How Does Cognitive Load Affect Students with ADHD?

Students with Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) often struggle to maintain focus for long periods. They are easily distracted and may find it difficult to organize their thoughts or complete tasks.

# When their cognitive load becomes too high:

* They lose focus more quickly.
* They make more mistakes.
* They take longer to complete tasks.
* They become mentally tired and frustrated.
* Their learning efficiency decreases.

As a result, even simple learning activities can become overwhelming if they require too much mental effort.

# How Does Cognitive Load Affect Students with Dyslexia?

Students with Dyslexia mainly face challenges related to reading and language processing. Reading, understanding text, and decoding words require much more mental effort compared to other learners.

# When cognitive load increases:

* Reading becomes slower and more difficult.
* Understanding lessons becomes harder.
* They become mentally exhausted while reading.
* Remembering information becomes challenging.
* Their confidence and motivation may decrease.

This makes learning more stressful, even though they are capable of understanding the concepts.

# What Problems Do Existing Methods Have?

Traditionally, cognitive load is measured using:

Teacher observations
Questionnaires
Psychological assessments
EEG (brainwave) sensors

These methods have several limitations:

* They are time-consuming.
* Results may depend on human observation.
* EEG equipment is expensive and uncomfortable.
* They cannot continuously monitor students during learning.

Because of these limitations, teachers often cannot identify when a student is becoming mentally overloaded.

# How Our Project Helps

Our project introduces a Cognitive Load Detector, an intelligent system that can automatically detect a student's cognitive load in real time.

Instead of using expensive EEG sensors, our system only requires a webcam and normal computer.

The system continuously observes:

Eye movements
Blink rate
Facial expressions
Head movements
Mouse movement
Typing behavior
Response time

Using Computer Vision (OpenCV) and Machine Learning algorithms like SVM and Random Forest, it analyzes these behaviors and predicts whether the student's cognitive load is Low, Medium, or High.

# Why Is This Useful?

If the system detects that a student's cognitive load is becoming too high, teachers can:

Give the student a short break.
Simplify the learning material.
Change the teaching pace.
Provide extra support before the student becomes frustrated.

This creates a more personalized and effective learning environment.
